- Behavior
Any type of human activity.


- Scientific Observation
A planned, systematic use of empirical evidence to study the world in an intersubjective fashion.


- Structuralism 
a psychological theory that tries to determine the basic elements, or building blocks, of behavior and mental processes. 



- Willhelm Wundt
The father of psychology, Wundt was a structuralist who set up a research laboratory in Germany in 1879.



- Functionalism
Perspective that mental processes cannot be broken down and instead are a steady flow of
consciousness


- William James
A student of Wilhelm Wundt, James was a functionalist and the first American to study psychology


(freudian slip)


- Psychodynamic Theory
A psychological theory of behavior that examines the role of the unconscious and internal conflicts.


- Psychoanalysis
Freudian psychodynamic therapy that attempts to uncover unconscious conflict



- Behaviorism
A theory in psychology that studies observable actions or behaviors rather than internal mental states.



- Ivan Pavlov
Developed the concept of Classical Conditioning.


- John watson
Founder of Behaviorism.


- B. F. Skinner
Founder of Operant Conditioning.


- Gestalt
Form, pattern, or whole. Thinking, learning, personality cannot be broken into parts—must be studied as
a complete unit, must understand relationships.


- Cognitive Theory
The study of people’s internal mental processes, like how they think, perceive, remember, and speak.

- Cognitive Behaviorism
A theory that combines both behavioral learning theories and cognitive theories. 


- Humanism
Believes in human potential, not drives; behavior is chosen, not determined—we have free will; seeks to
understand how we perceive the world and our place in it.


- Biological Perspective
Emphasis on explaining human behavior in terms of underlying physical, chemical, and biological
principles.


- Psychological Perspective
The view that behavior is shaped by psychological processes occurring internally at the level of the
individual.


- Sociocultural Perspective
The view that social and cultural contexts influence how people behave.
